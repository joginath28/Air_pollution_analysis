Insights:
1. PM2.5 levels exhibit a strong inverse relationship with visibility. As visibility increases, pollution levels decrease significantly. This indicates that particulate matter in the air directly reduces atmospheric clarity, making visibility a reliable proxy for air quality.
2. Rainfall shows a significant negative relationship with PM2.5 levels. This suggests that precipitation effectively removes airborne pollutants through wet deposition, leading to cleaner air conditions during and after rainfall events.
3. Higher wind speeds and gust intensity are associated with lower PM2.5 concentrations. This indicates that wind helps disperse pollutants, preventing their accumulation in a particular region and improving overall air quality.
4. Humidity has a moderate impact on pollution. And, Temperature has a limited direct influence; this demonstrates a weak relationship with PM2.5 levels.
5. Although temperature shows a weak direct relationship with PM2.5 in the regression model, time-series analysis indicates a clear seasonal inverse pattern. This suggests that temperature influences pollution indirectly through atmospheric conditions rather than acting as a primary driver.
6. Analysis of hourly and daily trends shows that PM2.5 levels vary over time, with noticeable peaks during certain periods(9 AM - 4 PM) of the day. These patterns are likely linked to human activities such as traffic and industrial operations. And, Pollution levels tend to differ less between weekdays and weekends.
7. There is substantial variation in PM2.5 concentrations across different countries. This highlights the impact of regional factors such as urbanization, industrialization, population density, and environmental regulations.
8. PM2.5 and PM10 are highly correlated. Other pollutants (NO₂, CO, SO₂) show interdependence, which suggests common sources of pollution such as: Vehicle emissions, Industrial output

Pollutant variables show strong interrelationships, with PM2.5 and PM10 being highly correlated due to their similar particulate nature, while gases like NO₂ and CO indicate common sources such as traffic and industrial emissions.
